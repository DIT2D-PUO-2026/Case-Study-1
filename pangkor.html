Pulau Pangkor

A quiet island in Perak known for beaches and seafood villages.

Why Visit?

Pulau Pangkor is a hidden gem off the coast of Perak, offering a peaceful escape from the hustle of city life. With its crystal-clear waters, charming fishing villages, and fresh-off-the-boat seafood, the island provides an authentic Malaysian coastal experience.

Enjoy quiet beaches without the crowds, watch hornbills in the wild, and soak in the laid-back atmosphere that makes Pangkor the perfect retreat for those seeking tranquillity and natural beauty.

Best Things To Do in Pangkor Island

Beaches & Water Sports

There are many beaches to visit on Pangkor Island. Coral Beach on the west side is considered the nicest beach on the island. You can rent a kayak from Coral Beach and paddle to the small island Pulau Giam, one of the best places to go snorkelling. Pangkor also offers banana boat rides, jet skis, and diving tours.

Wildlife Spotting

Since most of Pangkor is still jungle, you will spot wild animals during your visit. The island is especially known for its many Oriental Pied Hornbills, characterised by their massive yellow beaks. You'll also encounter families of monkeys hanging around rooftops and trees, fun to observe but keep your distance!

Rent a Scooter

Pangkor is not very big, so renting a scooter is the perfect way to get around. The roads are well paved and there isn't much traffic, making it great even for beginners. Spend a day exploring the east side of the island where most of the attractions are located. Scooter rental costs around 35 RM per day.

The Floating Mosque

Masjid Al-Badr, also known as Pangkor's floating mosque, is one of the best attractions on the island. It's a beautiful mosque built out over the ocean. Since it's a religious site, it's completely free to visit. Robes are available by the entrance if you need to cover up.

Fu Lin Kong Temple

One of the most amazing things to do on Pangkor Island is visiting the Taoist temple Fu Lin Kong. It's full of colourful figures and statues, the garden is built like a maze, and it has a pond full of turtles. Make sure to climb to the top for an amazing viewpoint over the island. Completely free of charge!

Fishermen's Village

Pangkor is mostly known for fishing, most of the east side is still one big fisherman village. Walk among the old houses, visit the dried seafood stalls, and eat some of the freshest fish you'll ever taste. Right next to the village you'll also find the Kaliamman Hindu Temple, free to visit.

Watch the Sunset

End your day by watching a breathtaking sunset from the west coast beaches. Coral Beach and Teluk Nipah are perfect spots to relax as the sun dips below the horizon, painting the sky in shades of orange and pink.
