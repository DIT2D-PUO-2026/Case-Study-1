Kuala Lumpur

Malaysia's capital city famous for skyscrapers and food streets.

Why Visit?

Kuala Lumpur is a dynamic capital city that seamlessly blends the old and new. The 451-metre Petronas Twin Towers and the 421-metre KL Tower define one of Southeast Asia's most recognisable skylines, while nearby streets are lined with colonial-era buildings, ornate Chinese temples, and colourful Hindu shrines such as the Sri Mahamariamman Temple.

Food is at the heart of KL's culture. Jalan Alor comes alive every night with hundreds of hawker stalls serving local favourites like char kuey teow, satay, and hokkien mee. For a more traditional experience, head to Kampung Baru to taste authentic Malay dishes like nasi lemak and rendang in a charming village setting right in the middle of the city.

Beyond the city centre, the limestone Batu Caves are home to a 140-year-old Hindu temple reached by 272 rainbow-painted steps is a must-visit. KL also offers world-class shopping at Bukit Bintang, lush green escapes at the KL Forest Eco Park, and a thriving arts scene in neighbourhoods like Zhongshan Building and REXKL.

Quick Facts

  • Best for: City life, food, and culture
  • Temperature: Around 27°C to 35°C
  • Recommended stay: 3 to 5 days
  • Top food: Nasi lemak, satay, and roti canai

Interesting Places to Visit in Kuala Lumpur

Iconic Landmarks

Petronas Twin Towers

The world's tallest twin structures, standing 88 stories with a skybridge connecting the two towers. Features an art gallery, shopping mall, underwater aquarium, and a science centre. The view at night is breathtaking against the KL skyline.

Menara KL Tower

Standing 421 metres tall, the KL Tower offers spectacular city views from 276 metres high. Its Islamic and Persian style architecture is stunning, and it features the tallest freestanding revolving restaurant, an amphitheatre, and cascading pools.

Sultan Abdul Samad Building

Built in 1897 in early Moorish-style architecture, this historic brick landmark sits in Merdeka Square. Its 41-metre high clock tower and Gothic and Western design influences make it a must-see attraction.

Dataran Merdeka

Kuala Lumpur's Merdeka Square is a grand field with lush green lawns and the Malaysian flag hoisted high in the centre. Once a famous cricket pitch, it became the venue for the annual Merdeka Parade.

National Monument

This 15-metre high bronze statue pays tribute to those who died in World War I. Seven soldiers depict qualities of strength, sacrifice, courage, leadership, vigilance and unity, surrounded by fountains and the ASEAN Sculpture Garden.

City Gallery

A tourist information centre featuring miniatures of famous KL landmarks, paintings and photographs steeped in the city's history. Don't miss the gift store with batik products and hand-woven items at great prices.

Temples & Mosques

Batu Caves

A 272-step trek leads to this century-old Hindu temple in limestone. The Cathedral Cave features a 100-metre high arched ceiling and several Hindu shrines, while the Art Gallery Cave and Museum Cave house statues and ancient paintings.

Sri Mahamariamman Temple

An extravagant Hindu temple decorated with Spanish and Italian tiles, precious stones and gold. Inside you'll find 228 Hindu idols and hand-painted motifs depicting Hindu folklore, all enveloped in tranquil incense and floral fragrances.

Thean Hou Temple

One of the largest temples in Southeast Asia, this six-tiered Buddhist place of worship is also called the Temple of the Goddess of Heaven. Features a huge octagonal hall, a modern Buddhist pagoda, and pleasing city views.

Jamek Mosque

The oldest place of worship in KL, offering a breathtaking view of the Klang River. Its Mughal and Moorish architecture features three domes and two brick minarets, plus an Islamic Experience Centre outside.

National Mosque of Malaysia

Spread across 13 acres with a main prayer hall featuring 48 mini domes. The 18-point main dome represents Malaysia's 13 states and the five pillars of Islam, with a stunning 73-metre high minaret, marble pools and fountains.

Sin Sze Si Ya Temple

Built in 1864, the oldest Taoist temple in KL serves as a cultural centre for the Chinese community. Students often pray here to Wenchang Dijun, the God of Education. Light candles and incense sticks in the temple grounds.

Nature & Parks

Perdana Botanical Gardens

Famous for the Orchid Gardens spanning 10,000 square metres with 800 orchid varieties, man-made fountains and rustic walking paths. The Hibiscus Garden showcases Malaysia's national flower in abundance.

KLCC Park

A 50-acre park surrounding the Petronas Towers, featuring the man-made Lake Symphony with regular water fountain shows. Enjoy reflecting pools, waterfalls, over 1,900 indigenous trees, and a children's playground.

KL Forest Eco Park

Formerly the Bukit Nanas Forest Reserve, this is one of the oldest forest reserves and the only tropical rainforest left in KLCC, sprawling across 110,000 square metres. Free entry.

Titiwangsa Lake Garden

A great recreational spot offering canoeing, aqua-biking, jogging tracks, horse riding, and a radio-controlled car racing track for kids. Grab food from lakeside stalls and enjoy a picnic by the water.

KL Bird Park

The world's largest covered bird park covering 20.9 acres, home to over 200 species including flamingos, hornbills, hawk eagles, and 20 species of parrots. Divided into 4 zones with free-flight aviaries.

Butterfly Park

Home to more than 6,000 types of butterflies among dense greenery, colourful flowers, cascading waterfalls and ponds. You'll also spot lovely Koi fish and freshwater turtles.

Taman Burung

Located in the serene Lake Gardens, home to 3,000 local and foreign birds over 21 acres. The world's largest free-flight walk-in aviary, where most birds roam freely in their natural habitat.

Museums & Culture

Islamic Arts Museum Malaysia

Southeast Asia's largest museum of Islamic Art, featuring over 7,000 artefacts divided into 12 themes. The building boasts a huge blue turquoise dome, glass walls, and ceilings with intricate patterns across 30,000 square metres.

Illusion 3D Art Museum

Explore 38 hand-painted masterworks on subjects including zombies, celebrities, escapades and more. Features an AR video studio where you can interact with virtual animals like dinosaurs, elephants and polar bears.

Petrosains, The Discovery Centre

A 7,000-square-metre interactive science centre in Petronas Towers with 11 showcases, exciting rides, a 3D mini theatre, a singing dinosaur, and a music and art zone. Great fun for kids and adults alike.

National Science Centre

A fun educational destination with exhibits covering a gamut of scientific theories. You're greeted by an overhead freshwater aquarium before proceeding to exhibits. Don't miss the science show beamed into the main dome.

Cultural Palace (Istana Budaya)

Malaysia's National Theatre and one of the world's top 10 most sophisticated theatres. Features a turquoise-blue tiled roof and hosts local and international performances, plus free live music and dance on Saturdays.

KL Upside Down House

A whimsical two-storey house built entirely upside down at KL Tower. The interiors and furniture are arranged on the ceiling, perfect for fun photos and a uniquely different experience.

Shopping & Markets

Chinatown

Known as the 'Bargain Hunter's Paradise', Chinatown on Petaling Street is a colourful, bustling neighbourhood offering everything from Chinese herbs to imitation goods, plus a vibrant night market.

Central Market

Built in 1918, this former wet market is now a handicrafts centre showcasing fine artisanal works. Find exquisite batik, silverware, baroque pearls, antiques, and local cuisine at the food court upstairs.

Petaling Street Market

A vibrant night market that satisfies all shopping needs, from the smallest personal items to household goods. Shops run till late at night, and the aroma of street food wafts through the air as you browse.

Pavilion KL

A shopping paradise spread over 1.37 million square feet of milky white marble, with 450 stores ranging from affordable to luxury brands. Features a Crystal Fountain wishing well, dining, and a 13-screen cinema.

Suria KLCC

An impressive 6-storey mall at the foot of the Petronas Towers, famous for haute couture stores like Chanel, Armani and Coach. Also home to Dewan Filharmonik Orchestra, Malaysia's first concert hall.

Bukit Bintang

KL's trendiest high-end shopping area with an array of shopping centres, five-star hotels, nightclubs and entertainment spots. Home to Pavilion KL and the famous Jalan Alor food street.

Connaught Night Market

A weekly Wednesday night market with over 700 stalls along a 2-kilometre road. Shop for accessories, bags, clothing and handicrafts at reasonable prices, and try the famous Laksa and Satay.

Brickfields (Little India)

KL's Little India neighbourhood with Indian stores and restaurants, loud Bollywood music, spices, sweets, sarees and garlands. Gorge on South Indian fare served on banana leaves, with shops open till late at night.

Theme Parks & Family Fun

Aquaria KLCC

One of the largest aquariums in Southeast Asia, home to over 150 species and 5,000 kinds of marine life including tiger sharks, seahorses, sea snakes, bright coral fish, and coral reefs.

Sunway Lagoon Theme Park

Features 5 zones: Amusement Park, Water Park, Wildlife Park, Extreme Park, and Scream Park. Enjoy water slides, a surf beach, river rides, a pirate ship, live shows and animal exhibits.

SuperPark Malaysia

An indoor theme park with eight adventure areas including a flying fox, kids gym, freestyle area, and kids' adventure city. Race your friends on pedal cars and tube slides for an adrenaline rush.

KL Tower Mini Zoo

Located inside KL Tower with eight zones replicating natural ecosystems. Home to meerkats, bat-eared foxes, raccoons, and Falabellas (the world's smallest horse species). Pet and feed the animals up close.

Zoo Negara

Spanning 115 acres, this zoological park houses 476 species of birds, reptiles, mammals, and amphibians in a 130-million-year-old forest. Enjoy trekking, boat rides, fishing, and wildlife viewing.

Kuala Gandah Elephant Sanctuary

A sanctuary in the village of Lanchang where orphaned elephants are raised with care. Over the past 25 years, staff have successfully relocated 400 wild elephants back into their natural habitat.

Farm In The City

A uniquely designed wildlife and nature park where visitors can pet, ride, and feed animals including ponies, pelicans, lemurs, otters, parrots, and porcupines in specially crafted habitats.

99 Wonderland Park

Malaysia's first urban-themed wildlife park with an open concept, allowing visitors to feed and interact with animals. Transforms into a glowing garden at night with beautiful scenery.

Kidzania

A small city with over 60 establishments and around 100 role-play activities for children aged 4 to 17. Kids engage in fun edutainment activities while parents can relax in the parents' lounge.

I-City Theme Park

Known for exciting rides, 3D and wax museums, a water park, and Malaysia's biggest snow park. At sunset, the artificial flora and fauna are illuminated with hundreds of LED lights, a dazzling spectacle.

District 21

A post-apocalyptic obstacle course theme park with ropes, platforms and chains. Features high-speed slides, trampolines and 11 obstacles with varying difficulty levels, great fun for the whole family.

Berjaya Times Square Theme Park

Malaysia's largest indoor theme park inside one of KL's biggest shopping malls. Features the Galaxy Station for adults and Fantasy Garden for kids, including the 800-metre long Supersonic Odyssey Roller Coaster.

Bukit Gambang Water Park

Award-winning water park featuring Malaysia's only wave pool with 8 different wave modes. Enjoy Penguin Island for children, the Lazy River for families, or the longest Family Raft ride.

Food & Nightlife

Jalan Alor

KL's hidden gem for street food, tucked behind Jalan Bukit Bintang. Famous for wonderful seafood and meat delicacies at cheap prices. Look for the fluorescent signboards and Chinese lanterns, open 24 hours.

Atmosphere 360

A sophisticated revolving restaurant located 282 metres above ground in Menara KL. Designed in the style of a spaceship, offering breathtaking city views and an unforgettable buffet dining experience.

The Row

Formerly Asian Heritage Row, now transformed into stylish bistros, restaurants and event spaces. Offers both local and European cuisines with live entertainment venues hosting musical shows and concerts.

Sky Bar

A glamorous cocktail lounge on the 33rd floor of the Traders Hotel, popular for its nightlife and exhilarating city views. Visit on weekdays for soft beats and Happy Hours with divine cocktails.

Luna Bar Rooftop

Atop the 34th floor of Pacific Regency Suites, one of KL's most well-known nightlife venues. Features a panoramic city view, swimming pool, a long bar, alfresco lounge, and indoor restaurant.

Pisco Bar

A posh hotspot in Bukit Bintang with eclectic decor and top DJs. Popular for live music performances, delicious cocktails, and unique tapas. Perfect for social gatherings and lively nights out.

Day Trips

Putrajaya

Malaysia's third Federal Territory spanning nearly 5,000 hectares with elegant European-styled streets. The well-structured city has its own waterway and modern architecture that makes for a pleasing visual treat.

KLCC (City Centre)

The heart of Kuala Lumpur and an ideal spot for entertainment, attractions, dining, and Malay culture. Often referred to as 'a city within a city' for all that it offers, shopping, nightlife, arts and culture.
