Langkawi

A tropical island paradise famous for beaches and the Sky Bridge.

Why Visit?

Langkawi is a duty-free archipelago of 99 islands surrounded by turquoise waters and lush rainforests. Whether you're soaring above the canopy on the iconic Sky Bridge, lounging on the powdery sands of Pantai Cenang, or exploring hidden mangrove rivers teeming with wildlife, Langkawi offers an unforgettable blend of adventure and relaxation.

Its rich legends, vibrant night markets, and stunning sunsets make it one of Malaysia's most beloved island getaways.

Interesting Places to Visit in Langkawi

Langkawi Cable Car (SkyCab)

Soar above the rainforest canopy on one of the steepest cable car rides in the world. The SkyCab whisks you up to 708 metres on Mat Cincang mountain, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the islands, sea, and jungle below.

Langkawi Sky Bridge

At the top cable car station, walk across this iconic 125-metre curved pedestrian bridge suspended 660 metres above sea level. The views from here are absolutely spectacular. You can even see Thailand on a clear day!

Kilim Karst Geoforest Park

Cruise through dramatic limestone formations and lush mangrove rivers on a boat tour. Spot eagles, monkeys, and monitor lizards in their natural habitat while exploring hidden caves and floating fish farms.

Underwater World Langkawi

One of the largest marine and freshwater aquariums in Southeast Asia, home to over 5,000 aquatic creatures. Walk through the mesmerising 15-metre underwater tunnel and meet penguins, seals, sharks, and rays.

Pantai Cenang

Langkawi's most popular beach stretches for nearly 2 kilometres with powdery white sand and calm turquoise waters. It's the perfect spot for swimming, jet-skiing, parasailing, and watching spectacular sunsets.

Island Hopping Tour

Hop between stunning islands including Pulau Dayang Bunting (Pregnant Maiden Island) with its freshwater lake, Pulau Singa Besar for eagle feeding, and Pulau Beras Basah with its pristine sandy beach.

Eagle Square (Dataran Lang)

Langkawi's most iconic landmark is a majestic 12-metre tall eagle sculpture perched at the waterfront. The name "Langkawi" itself is believed to derive from the Malay word for eagle (helang). A must-see photo spot!

Tanjung Rhu Beach

A serene and less crowded beach on the northern tip of Langkawi, framed by towering limestone karsts and casuarina trees. Its shallow, calm waters and soft sand make it ideal for families and a peaceful escape.

Telaga Tujuh Waterfalls (Seven Wells)

Seven cascading natural pools connected by a waterfall on Mat Cincang mountain. After a short jungle hike, cool off in the refreshing natural rock pools surrounded by lush tropical rainforest.

Langkawi Wildlife Park

Home to over 2,500 animals including flamingos, raccoons, macaws, and crocodiles. This interactive park lets visitors get up close with friendly animals through feeding sessions and hands-on encounters.

3D Art in Paradise

An interactive 3D art museum along Pantai Cenang where you become part of the artwork! With over 200 optical illusion paintings across multiple themed zones, it's a fun and creative experience for all ages.

Night Markets (Pasar Malam)

Langkawi's lively night markets rotate between different locations each day of the week. Sample delicious local street food like satay, grilled seafood, and fresh tropical fruit juices at bargain prices.

Duty-Free Shopping

As a duty-free island, Langkawi offers incredible deals on chocolate, alcohol, cosmetics, and electronics. Head to the Kuah town duty-free zone or Pantai Cenang shops for the best bargains.
